To calculate the Linguistic Quality Index (LQI), we aggregated four robust dimensions. Click below to see the exact formulas derived from our data processing.

Lexical Richness Robust vocabulary diversity (Herdan's C).
▼
log(Unique Words) / log(Total Words)

Justification: Standard Type-Token Ratio (Unique/Total) is biased against long texts. We used Herdan's C (Log-TTR) to ensure fair comparison between short comments and long rants.

Why it matters: It distinguishes between users who repeat the same keywords ("spam/circlejerk") and users who employ a varied, precise vocabulary.

Structural Complexity A balance of word length and sentence construction.
▼
(0.5 * Avg Word Len) + log(Avg Sentence Len)

Justification: Simple sentence length is noisy (a 50-word list of groceries is not "complex"). We created a composite score that rewards using longer, more complex words within structurally longer sentences.

Why it matters: This identifies "Elaborated Code"—language used to construct detailed arguments rather than quick, reactive firing.

Formality Index Objectivity vs. Subjectivity (Contextuality).
▼
Articles - Pronouns - (2 * Swearing) - Uppercase

Justification: Based on Heylighen & Dewaele (2002).
• (+) Articles (The/A): Indicate noun-heavy, objective descriptions.
• (-) Pronouns (I/You): Indicate subjective, chatty interaction.
• (-) Swearing/Caps: Heavily penalized markers of impulsivity.

Why it matters: It separates "Contextual" language (casual, face-to-face style) from "Formal" language (academic/professional style).

Cognitive Depth The presence of active reasoning.
▼
Mean(LIWC_CogMech + LIWC_Insight + LIWC_Cause)

Justification: We aggregate LIWC categories that track words related to processing information (think, know) and causality (because, hence, effect).

Why it matters: This distinguishes between descriptive storytelling or emotional venting and analytical reasoning.
